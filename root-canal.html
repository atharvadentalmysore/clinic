What is a Root Canal?

A root canal (or endodontic therapy) is a procedure to treat infection deep inside a tooth. When the soft tissue inside your tooth — called the pulp — becomes inflamed or infected due to deep decay, cracks, or trauma, a root canal removes the damaged pulp, cleans the internal canals, and seals the tooth to prevent reinfection.

The result? Your natural tooth is preserved, the pain stops, and with a protective crown, the tooth functions normally for years — often for life.

When Do You Need a Root Canal?

You may need root canal treatment if you experience:

  • Severe, persistent toothache — especially when biting or applying pressure
  • Prolonged sensitivity to hot or cold that lingers after the stimulus is removed
  • Darkening or discoloration of a tooth
  • Swelling and tenderness in the nearby gums
  • A recurring pimple-like bump on the gums (fistula)

If you notice any of these signs, do not delay. Early treatment prevents the infection from spreading and saves the tooth.

The Procedure: What to Expect

  1. Diagnosis: Digital X-ray and examination to assess the infection.
  2. Anesthesia: The tooth is numbed completely using local anesthesia for patient comfort.
  3. Access & Cleaning: The infected pulp is carefully removed and the canals are shaped and disinfected.
  4. Filling: The canals are sealed with biocompatible material.
  5. Crown: A custom crown is placed over the treated tooth for long-term protection.

Recovery and Aftercare

Most patients return to normal activities the same day. Mild tenderness for 2–3 days is normal and manageable with basic over-the-counter medication. Dr. Kishore provides detailed aftercare instructions and is available for follow-up queries.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is root canal treatment painful?

Modern root canal therapy at Atharva Dental is designed for patient comfort. Dr. Kishore G uses advanced local anesthesia and rotary instruments that are gentler than older methods. Most patients report that it feels similar to getting a regular filling.

How many visits does a root canal take?

Most root canals at our clinic are completed in 1 to 2 visits depending on the severity of infection. Simple cases can often be treated in a single appointment.

What happens if I delay root canal treatment?

Delaying treatment allows the infection to spread deeper into the tooth and bone. This can lead to abscess formation, severe swelling, bone loss, and eventually tooth loss. Early treatment saves both the tooth and the cost of more complex procedures.

How long does a root canal treated tooth last?

With proper care and a protective crown, a root canal treated tooth can last a lifetime. Dr. Kishore recommends a dental crown after the procedure for long-term structural integrity.
