What Are Dental Implants?

A dental implant is a small titanium post that is surgically placed into the jawbone beneath the gum line. Over several weeks, the bone naturally fuses with the titanium — a process called osseointegration — creating a rock-solid foundation. A custom-made ceramic crown is then attached to the implant, giving you a tooth that looks, feels, and functions like a natural one.

Unlike dentures which can slip and click, or bridges which require grinding down adjacent healthy teeth, implants are standalone replacements that preserve your remaining dental structure.

The Implant Process

  1. Consultation & Imaging: Thorough examination and digital scans to assess bone quality and plan placement.
  2. Implant Placement: The titanium post is precisely placed under local anesthesia. Most patients describe it as easier than an extraction.
  3. Healing Period: 3–6 months for the bone to fuse with the implant (you'll have a temporary restoration during this time).
  4. Crown Placement: A custom ceramic crown is fabricated and attached — your new tooth is complete.

Single Tooth vs. Multiple Teeth vs. Full Arch

Whether you're missing one tooth, several teeth, or need a full set, implants can be tailored to your needs. For patients missing all teeth, as few as 4–6 implants can support an entire arch of fixed teeth — eliminating dentures entirely.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long do dental implants last?

With proper care, dental implants can last 20–25 years or even a lifetime. The titanium post fuses permanently with your jawbone.

Is the implant procedure painful?

The procedure is performed under local anesthesia. Most patients report less discomfort than expected. Post-operative tenderness is mild and manageable.

Am I a candidate for dental implants?

Most adults with adequate jawbone density are candidates. Dr. Kishore evaluates each case with digital imaging. Bone grafting may help patients with some bone loss.
